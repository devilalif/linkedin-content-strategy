## Full Draft — Post 1: The Mid-Year AI Marketing Report Card

*Rationale: June 1 is the only day this post is perfectly timed. Every marketing leader is running a mid-year review. An honest, specific, first-person account — including failures — from someone running AI-driven marketing at scale is a post that gets saved, shared, and debated. ---

Six months into running AI-driven marketing at Vantage Circle.

Here's my honest report card — what worked, what didn't, and the one thing I wasn't expecting.

---

**What worked:**

Topic clustering at scale.

AI maps the content landscape across hundreds of keywords faster than any human team can. We use it to find gaps, cluster related ideas, and brief the editorial calendar. Time saved: roughly 80%. Quality of the brief: higher, because the pattern recognition is better than a spreadsheet.

Automated performance reporting.

What used to be a two-hour Monday morning exercise — pulling data, building the summary, writing the narrative — is now 15 minutes of verification. The AI generates the weekly report. I verify the interpretation. This alone recovered nearly a full day per week.

---

**What flopped:**

Fully AI-generated first drafts.

The voice was wrong every time. Not because the AI was bad — because our content has a specific editorial stance, and that stance isn't in a prompt. Reworking a bad AI draft often took longer than writing from scratch. We stopped doing it.

AI-generated CTAs.

We tested AI-written calls-to-action against human-written ones for three months. Human-written CTAs outperformed by 34% on click-through. The AI versions were technically correct and completely unconvincing. Conversion copy is not a writing problem. It's a psychological one.

Trying to automate strategy.

AI cannot read the room. It doesn't know what your leadership just decided, what a competitor just launched, or how your audience mood has shifted. We learned this the hard way when automated content planning ignored context that changed everything. Strategy stays human.

---

**The one thing I wasn't expecting:**

The best use of AI wasn't creating content.

It was listening.

AI-powered brand monitoring — tracking how Vantage Circle gets referenced in LLM responses, AI search environments, and industry conversations — surfaced insights about our content authority that traditional analytics never could.

When ChatGPT answers a question about employee engagement software and names a competitor instead of us, that's a strategic gap. AI helped us see those gaps for the first time.

The game in H2 isn't publishing more.

It's becoming the source that AI systems reference when they answer questions your buyers are asking.

---

Six months in, the honest verdict:

AI is not an upgrade to how you already work.

It's a restructure of what the work actually is.

The teams that figure that out in H2 will be the ones that compound.
