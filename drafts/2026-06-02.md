## Full Draft — Post 3: The Nutrition Rule for Knowledge Workers

*Rationale: The most overdue idea in the entire backlog — planned since Week 1, carried through five sessions without a full draft. ---

I used to think nutrition was for athletes.

Then I realized: anyone whose job requires sustained thinking is an athlete.

The difference is, most knowledge workers have no nutrition strategy at all.

---

In competition prep, I learned something counterintuitive.

The variable that separates consistent performers from erratic ones isn't their supplement stack.

It's not their calorie count. It's not even their meal timing.

It's **protein distribution** across the day.

As a NESTA Sports Nutrition Specialist, I've tracked this pattern across training clients and in my own prep cycles:

Athletes who spread protein across four or five eating occasions perform more consistently — not just physically.

Cognitively too.

Decision-making under pressure. Sustained focus in hour five of a long workday. Stress tolerance when the deadline moves or the brief changes at the last minute.

All of it degrades when protein drops.

---

Most professionals eat one solid protein meal — usually dinner — and run on carbs, snacks, and caffeine the rest of the day.

That's not a nutrition plan.

That's an energy crash in slow motion.

---

Here's what I actually do — on a real workday, not a competition day:

**Morning:** 30–35g protein with the first meal. Non-negotiable. This sets the blood sugar baseline for the next four to five hours and gives the brain a stable substrate to work from.

**Midday:** Another 25–30g. This is where it falls apart for most people — the lunch that becomes "just a salad," or the meal skipped entirely because of a back-to-back. This single gap tanks afternoon output more than any distracting notification ever will.

**Evening:** Eat what you like. By this point, the work is done.

---

The rule isn't complicated:

Get 25–30g of protein in your first two meals, consistently, before you optimize anything else.

Not keto. Not fasting. Not a stack of supplements.

Protein. Early. Distributed.

That's the foundation. Everything else is tuning.

---

If your afternoon runs on willpower and a third coffee — this is where I'd start before changing anything else.

What does your actual nutrition look like before noon? I'm genuinely curious what most people are eating.
