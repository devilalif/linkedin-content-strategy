## Full Draft — Post 2: Seven Years, Same Company

*Rationale: Personal story is the most underserved pillar — one full draft across four weeks of planning. June is peak career reflection season: mid-year reviews are wrapping, LinkedIn is flooded with new-role announcements, and ambitious professionals are quietly asking themselves whether to stay or go. This post is the direct counter-narrative to LinkedIn's job-hopping culture, grounded in Shah's specific 7-year trajectory at Vantage Circle across three completely different roles. High save and comment potential.*

---

Everyone told me to jump companies if I wanted to grow.

I didn't.

Seven years later, I'm glad I ignored that advice.

When I joined Vantage Circle, I was a content writer.

No strategy title. No manager role. No AI anything.

Over the next seven years, I moved from content marketing → digital marketing → AI marketing.

Same company. Completely different job.

Here's what nobody told me staying would actually give me:

**Real experiments, not just pitches.**
Job-hoppers build strategies and leave before they're proven. I got to execute, break, fix, and own results across years — not quarters. That's a different kind of education.

**Trust that compounds.**
I've worked with the same colleagues and leaders long enough to disagree without it becoming political. That's not a soft skill. That's leverage.

**The long view on what actually works.**
Tactics that looked brilliant at 6 months failed at month 18. Approaches that got dismissed early quietly outperformed everything. You only see that if you stay.

---

I won't pretend it was easy.

In years 3 and 4, I watched colleagues who'd left post promotions, company launches, new cities.

I was still iterating on the same content operation.

I stayed because I wasn't finished.

The systems I was building weren't done. The AI transition we were running hadn't paid off yet. Leaving would have meant trading real depth for the appearance of momentum.

---

Seven years in, I'm running AI-driven marketing for a product that reaches millions.

I didn't need a new company to grow.

I needed the patience to grow where I was planted.

---

Wherever you are in your career right now — are you building depth, or chasing the next thing because staying feels harder?

I'm genuinely curious what's keeping people.
