Q2 ends in two days.

Before I start H2, I want to document what I actually learned — not what I planned to learn.

Six months of running an AI marketing function at scale will dismantle a few things you thought were working.

---

**What I got wrong in H1:**

**Speed is not the advantage anymore.**

Every serious marketing team has AI tools. The competitive edge has shifted — not to who produces more, but to who understands more.

Production velocity is now table stakes.

Depth of insight is not.

**The "informed generalist" take has been commoditised.**

AI can write a well-researched, credible-sounding take on almost any industry topic. Fluently. At scale. Overnight.

What still cuts through: original research, proprietary data, and a specific human perspective readers can't get anywhere else.

"Here's what the trend shows" is done. "Here's what our data actually showed" still works.

**Brand mention in AI responses is a better pipeline signal than organic rank.**

We watch what gets cited in AI-generated answers — not just what ranks on page one.

The gap between the two showed us where our authority was real and where we'd just accumulated rankings without earning them.

---

**My three bets for Q3:**

**1. Original research over volume.**

One data point we generated ourselves is worth more than twelve takes on someone else's study.

**2. Author authority over content authority.**

Consistent first-person POV, bylines, a perspective readers can predict — because AI can't replicate a specific human voice that's been showing up for years.

**3. Third-party signal investment.**

Panels. Roundtables. Guest contributions. The places where external voices validate the expertise — because that's increasingly what gets cited when AI synthesises the conversation in our space.

---

Q3 starts in two days.

The teams that compound into Q4 won't be the ones that pushed hardest in H1.

They'll be the ones that were honest about what H1 actually taught them — and changed their bets accordingly.
